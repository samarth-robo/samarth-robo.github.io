Samarth Brahmbhatt

I am a Research Scientist in the Intel Labs, working on contact-rich robot manipulation, navigation, and locomotion.

I finished a post-doc with Vladlen Koltun at Intel in April 2022. My PhD work in Robotics at Georgia Tech was advised by James Hays. I also worked closely with Charlie Kemp. My PhD thesis was on observing and predicting hand-object interaction during human grasping, especially from the contact perspective.

I have a Masters degree in Robotics from the University of Pennsylvania, where I had the pleasure to work with Kostas Daniilidis. Before that, I got a bachelor's degree in electronics and communication engineering from Nirma University, India.
